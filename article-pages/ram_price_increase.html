Why RAM Prices Are Skyrocketing Right Now

Alex Chen

Senior Hardware Analyst â€¢ Dec 12, 2025

6 min read

If you've been planning a PC build in late 2024 or 2025, you might have noticed a painful trend: memory kits that cost $90 a few months ago are now pushing $130. Here is the technical breakdown of why this is happening.

1. The AI Gold Rush (HBM vs. DDR)

The biggest culprit is actually Artificial Intelligence. Companies like Nvidia need massive amounts of HBM3 and HBM3e (High Bandwidth Memory) for their AI GPUs.

Major manufacturers like SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron have shifted their production lines away from standard consumer DDR5 to produce HBM because the profit margins on AI memory are significantly higher.

"Every wafer allocated to HBM is one less wafer for consumer DDR5. Supply is shrinking while demand remains steady."

2. Strategic Production Cuts

Throughout 2023, RAM prices were historically low due to an oversupply. To stop losing money, the "Big Three" manufacturers intentionally cut production utilization rates by up to 50%.

These cuts have now successfully drained the excess inventory. With inventory levels normalized and production lines still not running at 100% capacity for consumer chips, prices naturally climb.

3. The Shift to DDR5 Standard

As Intel's Core Ultra and AMD's Ryzen 9000 series push DDR5 as the exclusive standard, the demand for old DDR4 is dropping, but the demand for high-speed DDR5 (6000MHz+) is spiking. Manufacturing yields for these high-speed bin modules are improving, but not fast enough to match the holiday and upgrade season demand.

Should you buy now?

Analysts predict prices will continue to rise by another 15-20% through Q1 2026. If you are sitting on the fence for a build, locking in your memory kit now might save you $30-$50 compared to waiting for next month.
